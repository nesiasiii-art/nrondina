# Transfer a Caller to Customer Satisfaction Survey

**Target audience:** All (COMPANY) support agents

After the end of every call, the (COMPANY) user support system transfers phone callers who called via the Help Hotline to a Customer Satisfaction Survey. This happens regardless of the issue type, except in certain situations like the caller declining the survey or an interrupted call. This article explains the Customer Satisfaction Survey transfer guidelines and FAQs.

### What are Warm & Cold Transfers?
If a caller needs assistance from third-party support, you can do either a warm or cold transfer depending on the situation.
- **Warm transfers:** Reach out to a third-party agent with the caller on the line.
	- Inform the third-party agent about the issue before you hang up.
- **Cold transfer:** Reach out to a third-party agent, but do not stay on the line.

## Discuss the Customer Satisfaction Survey with the Caller
- Make sure to converse with a friendly and positive tone.
- Let the caller know why we encourage them to take the survey. The survey lets us know where and how we can improve our services in the future.
- Keep your message consistent, and shouldn't change drastically from call to call.
- Do not overly personalize the discussion, as the survey is about the caller's overall support experience.

## Frequently Asked Questions
**What if a caller's issue isn't related to the product I'm supporting?**
- Only troubleshoot issues related to the products you support. To be respectful of the caller's time, if you're not trained on the specific issue:
	1. Tell the customer that you don't support the product that they're having an issue with.
	2. Transfer them to the correct support team.
		- Give them the best resource related to the issue with the Support Troubleshooter tool.

**What if the caller asks what the survey is about?**
- Use this sample script:
	> *"The survey will ask about your experience with our support. It's very important for us to listen to our customers in order to improve our services. We highly appreciate your feedback."

**What if the caller asks how to take the survey?**
- Use this sample script:
	> *"Are you willing to stay on the call to take a short survey about your experience with this call?"*
